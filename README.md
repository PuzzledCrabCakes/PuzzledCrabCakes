# Hello,
I am a Computer Science student who made the journey from creative pursuits to concrete problem-solving. I used to be all about music production, but somewhere I decided I actually like ideas
that are backed by numbers and analysis.

## What I'm into at the moment
- Data Analysis and ML: I love finding patterns in data and turning raw information into real solutions.
- Databases & System Architecture: There is something really satisfying about designing ways to store and retrieve information, big or small.
- Network Security: I have been wanting to understand how systems work under the hood so I can help protect them.
- Python Development: Who doesn't like snakes?

## How I learn
I bounce around too much for my own good. I move through things quickly before diving deep. But I do find analogies to be helpful for understanding new concepts
I am more interested in building things that *work* than things that look pretty. I will take a fully functioning terminal app over a pretty UI design any day. 
When approaching coding problems, I sometimes still think like a music producer and approach it like I am crate digging. Trying to feel out and find the right samples and combining them in new ways to create something that just feels right.

Indiana Jones doesn't like snakes actually.

I am working through my CS coursework while trying to build practical skills in data mining, statistical analysis, and database design.
